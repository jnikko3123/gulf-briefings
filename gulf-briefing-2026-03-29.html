72-Hour Escalation Outlook

The most immediate risk is Houthi resumption of Red Sea commercial shipping attacks. Following their 28 March missile barrage on Israel, Houthi officials told the Associated Press that the first maritime strike could come imminently. If confirmed, Bab el-Mandeb closes as a bypass route for Gulf oil, compounding the Hormuz blockade. Simultaneously, Trump's energy infrastructure strike pause expires 6 April, and Iran's Defence Council has warned that any strike on Iranian coastal territory triggers mine-laying across all Gulf sea lanes, a step that would extend disruption far beyond the Hormuz chokepoint. Watch the April 5 OPEC+ emergency session for any emergency production ceiling adjustment. Kataib Hezbollah's conditional ceasefire on the US Embassy in Baghdad is fragile; any perceived violation could restart drone strikes and pull Iraq deeper into the conflict, directly threatening Kirkuk-Ceyhan pipeline flows. Oman is the sole active diplomatic back-channel; any Muscat announcement this week signals the last viable off-ramp before further escalation.

Signal vs. Noise
ACTIONABLE SIGNALS
  • Iran allowing humanitarian and fertilizer shipments through Hormuz (UN brokered, 27 Mar): signals partial diplomatic flexibility and a potential framework for broader reopening.
  • Trump extending energy strike pause to 6 April: active negotiations are real; a deal remains possible before that deadline.
  • Oman FM active in back-channel communications: Muscat has historically delivered US-Iran breakthroughs; any sudden Oman travel by US envoy is a leading indicator of progress.
  • Ras Tanura and Yanbu both operational: Saudi core export capacity is intact; Abqaiq not yet struck, meaning maximum disruption scenario has not materialized.
  • Kataib Hezbollah ceasefire on US Embassy holding since 19 Mar: reduces probability of direct US-Iraq escalation in near term.
  • OPEC+ emergency session 5 April: any decision to waive production ceilings would moderate price pressure and reduce economic incentive for Iranian escalation.
NOISE / DO NOT OVERREACT
  • Daily Houthi rhetoric about joining the war: the 28 Mar missile launch is real, but Houthi leadership has not yet issued a formal maritime attack order; wait for confirmed ship strikes before pricing in full Red Sea closure.
  • Iranian mine-laying threat: a deterrent posture, not an imminent operational act; mine deployment requires coastal access that US naval forces are actively contesting.
  • Reports of Abqaiq being "next target": Iran has threatened Abqaiq repeatedly without striking; its value as a deterrent threat may exceed its value as an actual target.
  • Trump "considering taking over the Strait of Hormuz": negotiating leverage statement, not an operational order; no CENTCOM movement toward Hormuz seizure confirmed.
  • $120/bbl headline risk: oil touched $119.80 on 8 Mar and pulled back; current $114 reflects war premium, not a supply-destruction scenario where Abqaiq and Ras Laffan are both offline simultaneously.

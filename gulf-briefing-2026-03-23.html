Actionable Signals

  • Trump's 48-hour Hormuz ultimatum is the single most important near-term trigger. Expiry without compliance means US naval action against IRGC assets.
  • Houthis awaiting Iranian signal to resume Red Sea attacks. Any maritime incident near Bab el-Mandeb would confirm activation of a second front.
  • Iraq airspace closure extended to March 26, signaling expectation of continued aerial combat over Iraqi territory.
  • Saudi Arabia's "trust is gone" statement marks a diplomatic shift; watch for any move from defensive to active coalition participation.
  • Ras Laffan damage timeline (3-5 years) will reshape global LNG markets for the rest of the decade regardless of ceasefire timing.
  • Iran's selective Hormuz passage policy creates a two-tier shipping regime; Chinese and Indian tankers may continue transiting while Western vessels cannot.

Noise to Filter Out

  • Social media claims of nuclear strikes or radiation events. No verified nuclear detonation has occurred. Natanz was hit with conventional weapons.
  • Unverified casualty numbers circulating on Telegram channels. Stick to official government and hospital reports.
  • "Gulf NATO" rhetoric is aspirational signaling, not an operational alliance. No command structure or treaty exists.
  • Reports of Iranian submarines in the Gulf of Oman. Iran's submarine fleet has limited deep-water capability and poses minimal threat to US carrier groups.
  • Brent crude "heading to $200" predictions from speculative traders. SPR releases and demand destruction will cap prices below that level.
  • Claims that Oman has "switched sides." Muscat's diplomatic channel with Tehran is consistent with decades of Omani foreign policy.

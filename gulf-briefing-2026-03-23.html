72-Hour Escalation Outlook

Three triggers to watch over the next 72 hours. First, Houthi action: a senior Houthi official confirmed they are weighing a Bab el-Mandeb blockade targeting ships from nations involved in hostilities against Iran. Any closure would compound the Hormuz chokepoint crisis and push Brent well above $120. Second, Trump signaled he is considering "winding down" operations, but Iran flatly rejects ceasefire talks, calling the US fully responsible. A unilateral US drawdown without Iranian de-escalation could embolden proxy attacks on Gulf infrastructure. Third, Iraqi militia activity is surging: 50+ claimed attacks last weekend, including drone strikes on US bases in Erbil. Escalation from the Iraq theater could drag Baghdad into the conflict formally and threaten southern Iraqi oil fields that are already running at 30% capacity.

Houthi political bureau explicitly discussing Bab el-Mandeb blockade. A closure would create a second chokepoint crisis, cutting Red Sea trade.
Iran's new Supreme Leader hinting at "new fronts." This likely means activating Houthi maritime operations within days to weeks.

Hormuz transit improving slightly (8 non-Iranian vessels on one recent day). Iran may be selectively allowing traffic to manage escalation optics.
Trump considering "winding down" operations. Any US drawdown changes Gulf security calculus and may accelerate GCC bilateral talks with Tehran.
QatarEnergy's 5-year LNG repair timeline creates structural supply deficit for Asian gas markets. Watch for spot LNG price spikes.

Noise to Filter

Social media claims of imminent Hormuz "reopening." Data shows 13 vessels/day, far from normal 138. No systemic change yet.
Reports of Gulf states "entering the war." All five tracked countries maintain defensive posture and insist this is not their war.
Goldman Sachs projection of oil in the $70s by Q4 2026. This assumes Hormuz recovery starting April, which has no supporting diplomatic evidence.
Oman "congratulating" Iran's new Supreme Leader framed as alliance shift. Oman is maintaining its traditional mediator role, not aligning with Tehran.
Claims of Iranian naval "blockade." Iran is attacking ships, not running a formal blockade. The distinction matters for international maritime law and insurance classification.

72-Hour Escalation Outlook

The next 72 hours hinge on whether Trump's 5-day pause on strikes against Iranian power plants holds, and whether backchannel talks produce any verifiable de-escalation. Tehran's denial of negotiations suggests the pause is fragile. If talks collapse by March 28, expect US strikes on Iran's power grid, which Tehran has warned would trigger "complete closure" of Hormuz, including to neutral shipping. Watch for: (1) Houthi activation in the Red Sea, which could open a second chokepoint crisis; (2) further Iranian ballistic missile salvos against Gulf capitals, particularly Riyadh and Abu Dhabi; (3) any movement on Kataeb Hezbollah's conditional ceasefire in Baghdad; (4) Brent price reaction if the pause extends or collapses. Downside scenario: simultaneous Hormuz total closure and Red Sea Houthi resumption would trap ~20M bpd of supply.

Diplomacy & Leader Messaging

  • Saudi Arabia: FM Prince Faisal bin Farhan says trust in Tehran is "completely shattered." Riyadh reserves the right to take military action. Two ballistic missiles launched toward Riyadh on March 23; one intercepted, one landed in uninhabited area.
  • Iran: Tehran denies any talks with the US. Vowed to "completely close" Hormuz if power plants are struck. New Supreme Leader (post-Khamenei) messaging remains defiant. Over 1,047 civilians killed including 214 children.
  • Qatar: Expelled Iranian military/security personnel (24-hour deadline, March 19). FM called for de-escalation and return to negotiations. Condemned Iranian strikes on Qatari territory as sovereignty violation.
  • Oman: Sultan Haitham maintains mediation posture. FM Al-Busaidi says "off-ramps available" and calls for immediate ceasefire. Dismayed that strikes undermined "active negotiations" in Muscat. Three Omanis killed in Iranian strikes.
  • UAE: Absorbed major Iranian drone and missile salvos. French military base in Abu Dhabi hit (material damage only). Aligned with Saudi position supporting continued US strikes on Iran.
  • United States: Trump delayed strikes on Iranian power plants for 5 days. Claims "very good and productive conversations." CENTCOM: 9,000+ Iranian targets hit, 140+ naval vessels destroyed, 9,000+ combat sorties flown.

Oil Infrastructure Risk

  • Ras Tanura (Saudi): World's largest refinery. Hit by two Iranian drones on March 2. Intercepted but debris caused fire. Shut for ~1 week, now reopened. Operating at reduced capacity as alternate export routes established.
  • Safaniya/Zuluf (Saudi offshore): Both fields shut down. Saudi production dropped ~2M bpd to ~8M bpd. Storage terminals backing up with no export routes.
  • Abqaiq (Saudi): Processing plant on heightened alert. No confirmed strikes but within Iranian missile range.
  • Ras Laffan (Qatar): Iranian drones targeted QatarEnergy facility. Damage assessment unclear; LNG export disruption confirmed.
  • Port of Duqm (Oman): Multiple Iranian drones struck fuel tanks and a tanker at the port.
  • Ruwais/Fujairah (UAE): Under Patriot and THAAD coverage. No confirmed hits but Fujairah anchoring area crowded with 150+ vessels unable to transit Hormuz.
  • Kharg Island (Iran): Iran's main export terminal. Subject to US/Israeli strikes. Export capacity severely degraded.
  • Net impact: ~10M bpd offline. IEA calls this the largest oil supply disruption in recorded history.

Shipping & Chokepoints

  • Strait of Hormuz: Effectively closed. IRGC declared closure March 2. Selective passage allowed for China, India, Turkey, Pakistan-bound vessels. Only 16 AIS-visible crossings in past 7 days (vs. 700+ pre-war). Tanker traffic down ~97%.
  • Insurance market: War risk premiums have made transit commercially unviable. Lloyd's and major underwriters refusing coverage for Hormuz passage.
  • Red Sea / Bab el-Mandeb: Elevated risk, currently quiet. Houthis paused attacks since war began. US Operation Rough Rider striking Houthi positions since March 15 (53 killed on day one). Houthi leadership threatens "naval blockade" of aggressor nations but has not acted.
  • Suez Canal: Operational. Traffic reduced due to upstream Hormuz closure and Red Sea uncertainty. Some carriers rerouting via Cape of Good Hope.
  • Carrier decisions: Maersk, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd suspended all Hormuz and Red Sea transits. Trump announced US to offer insurance guarantees and tanker escorts, no implementation yet.

Proxy & Militia Activity

  • Iraq (Kataeb Hezbollah / PMF): "Islamic Resistance in Iraq" claimed 50+ attacks over one weekend using missiles and UAVs. 60+ PMF fighters killed, 100+ wounded in coalition airstrikes since Feb 28. Kataeb Hezbollah declared conditional ceasefire on US Embassy Baghdad (March 20): demands end to Israeli strikes on Beirut, end to strikes on Iraqi residential areas, CIA withdrawal from Iraqi territory.
  • Lebanon (Hezbollah): Full-scale war with Israel since March 2. 1,000+ killed, ~1M displaced (20% of Lebanon's population). Israeli ground operations in southern Lebanon since March 16. Hezbollah launching daily rocket and missile barrages into northern Israel.
  • Yemen (Houthis): Strategic pause on Red Sea shipping attacks. Awaiting Iranian signal to resume. US Operation Rough Rider pounding Houthi positions across seven provinces. Houthi leadership rhetoric escalatory but actions restrained; self-preservation amid Yemeni civil war dynamics likely a factor.
  • Iran domestic: Credible reports of Iraqi militia members (including Kataeb Hezbollah) deployed inside Iran to suppress protests. Internal unrest complicating Iran's war posture.

72-Hour Escalation Outlook

Iran's rejection of the US 15-point ceasefire plan on March 25 narrows the diplomatic window. Tehran's counteroffer, demanding reparations and sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz, is a non-starter for Washington. Watch for Iranian strikes on additional GCC energy infrastructure if the US expands strike packages inside Iran. The Houthis remain the biggest wild card: Abdul Malik Al-Houthi stated Thursday that the group will activate militarily if "developments require it," and the US issued a formal Bab El-Mandeb warning on March 27. Any fresh Houthi attack on a tanker in the Red Sea restarts the 2024 shipping disruption cycle with far less global slack. In Iraq, Kataib Hezbollah struck Iraqi CTS and INIS on March 21-22, signaling displacement of attacks rather than deescalation. In Lebanon, IDF ground operations in the south since March 16 are intensifying. Watch: IDF targeting of Beirut, Houthi activation, and any Iranian strike on Abqaiq or UAE infrastructure.

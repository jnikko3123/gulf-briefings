72-Hour Escalation Outlook

The OPEC+ meeting on 5 April is the near-term pivot point: member states must decide whether to add 206,000 b/d as planned while actual export volumes remain constrained by Hormuz disruption. Any push by Iran-aligned voices to hold production will signal coordinated economic pressure on the West. Watch Oman's foreign ministry for signaling from Tehran. Kataeb Hezbollah's five-day ceasefire pause expired around 24 March; renewed drone attacks on US bases in Iraq are likely before the weekend. Saudi Arabia's Pakistan-Turkey-Egypt mediation track has produced no deliverable yet. The Houthis have now resumed missile attacks on Israel (28 Mar) and Iran is actively pushing them to reopen Red Sea shipping attacks. Any confirmed merchant vessel strike in the Red Sea before 5 April would trigger immediate insurance and routing decisions by major carriers, further compressing cargo capacity through the Cape route. The most dangerous 72-hour scenario: an Iranian drone or missile strike on Al Dhafra Air Base or NSA Bahrain that kills US personnel, triggering a direct US escalation inside Iran.

Diplomacy & Leader Messaging
Saudi Arabia
Mediating

Crown Prince MBS is pursuing a Pakistan-Turkey-Egypt diplomatic track and conducting daily backchannel calls with Tehran. The Kingdom is simultaneously accelerating military preparations for a prolonged conflict. Saudi officials have endorsed Pakistan as the regional mediator.

Iran
Belligerent

Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in opening US-Israeli strikes on 28 Feb. Iran's surviving leadership continues retaliation operations while selectively engaging Oman's backchannel. No senior leader has publicly committed to ceasefire talks.

Oman
Neutral Channel

Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi remains the sole active diplomatic bridge. Muscat is the only Gulf capital not struck by Iranian missiles. FM Al-Busaidi has expressed dismay at the conflict and urges the US to avoid further escalation. Oman has kept the line to Tehran open.

Qatar
Supporting

Doha is co-sponsoring the Turkey-Egypt mediation framework. Al Udeid Air Base remains the operational hub for US air operations. Qatar is walking a careful line between hosting US forces and maintaining diplomatic relations with regional actors.

UAE
Targeted

The UAE was directly struck by Iranian missiles and drones in February-March 2026. Abu Dhabi is participating in GCC coordinated responses. Al Dhafra Air Base continues to operate but is on heightened force protection status. UAE has not publicly joined combat operations.

Oil Infrastructure Risk
Ras Tanura (Saudi Arabia)
Restored / Elevated Risk

Attacked by two Iranian drones on 2 Mar; both intercepted, minor debris fire. Second drone attempt on 4 Mar intercepted with no damage. Refinery reopened 13 Mar. Aramco has rerouted export flows as contingency. Security posture remains elevated.

Abqaiq (Saudi Arabia)
Watch

No confirmed attack in 2026. Remains the highest-value single target in global oil infrastructure, processing 7% of world supply. Saudi forces have reinforced air defense around the facility following the Ras Tanura attack.

East-West Pipeline (Saudi Arabia)
Operational

Saudi Arabia is moving 7 Mb/d via this pipeline as a Hormuz bypass, exporting through Yanbu on the Red Sea. This is the key variable enabling Saudi exports to continue despite Hormuz disruption.

Ras Laffan (Qatar)
Elevated Risk

The world's largest LNG export terminal continues to operate. No confirmed attack. LNG tanker operators are requesting naval escort through Hormuz. Qatar has not announced production cuts.

Kharg Island (Iran)
Under Threat

Iran's primary crude export terminal (handles ~90% of Iranian exports). US and Israeli strikes have reportedly targeted associated pipeline infrastructure. Iran's export capacity is severely reduced.

Fujairah / Ruwais (UAE)
Contingency Active

Fujairah is receiving rerouted tankers that previously transited Hormuz. Habshan-Fujairah pipeline is active at capacity. UAE is positioning Fujairah as an alternative export hub for the duration of the crisis.

Shipping & Chokepoints
Strait of Hormuz
Contested

Iran deployed asymmetric naval assets (fast boats, mines) following the 28 Feb strikes. Attacks confirmed: tanker west of Sharjah (explosion), tanker north of Muscat (fire), vessel NW of Mina Saqr (projectile hit). Major shipping companies have halted regular transits. US 5th Fleet providing escort for select military logistics.

Red Sea / Bab el-Mandeb
High Risk

Houthis resumed missile attacks on Israel on 28 Mar and are threatening to reopen merchant vessel strikes. Iran is actively pushing them to do so. Major carriers have rerouted around Cape of Good Hope since early March. Suez Canal transit volumes have collapsed.

Suez Canal
Restricted

Canal is physically open but effectively bypassed by major container carriers due to Houthi threat. Egyptian revenue from canal tolls has fallen sharply. Warships and military logistics still transit under escort.

Carrier Decisions
Rerouting

Lloyd's List and multiple container carriers have confirmed Gulf exits and Red Sea avoidance. Box shipping has effectively exited Hormuz. LNG operators are requesting naval escort or bypassing entirely. Insurance war-risk premiums have spiked 15-fold since 28 Feb.

Proxy & Militia Activity
Kataeb Hezbollah (Iraq)
Ceasefire Lapsing

Joined the conflict in early March with drone strikes on Baghdad area, targeting Victory Base/BIAP. The US Embassy in Baghdad was hit by drone on 18 Mar. Kataeb Hezbollah declared a five-day ceasefire on 19 Mar after senior commander Abu Ali al-Askari was killed on 16 Mar. The ceasefire has expired; renewed attacks are expected. Also attacked energy infrastructure in northern Iraq, Jordan, Kuwait, and Saudi Arabia.

Asaib Ahl al-Haq (Iraq)
Active

Joined Kataeb Hezbollah in attacks on US assets. Harakat Hezbollah al-Nujaba also announced participation. Coalition of Iraqi Shia militias is operating with less central coordination than in prior campaigns, suggesting independent operational decisions.

Houthis (Yemen)
Escalating

Resumed ballistic missile attacks on Israel on 28 Mar with a barrage of 40+ rockets. Iran is actively pushing the Houthis to reopen maritime attacks on Red Sea shipping. The Houthis threatened to create a "second chokepoint" alongside Hormuz. A confirmed Red Sea merchant strike is the primary near-term trigger to watch.

Hezbollah (Lebanon)
Full Engagement

Hezbollah is in active combat with Israeli forces following the 16 Mar ground invasion of southern Lebanon. Israeli strike on Beirut on 1 Apr killed a senior Hezbollah commander. Lebanese PM Nawaf Salam has formally banned Hezbollah military operations, but the group has refused to comply. Death toll in Lebanon exceeds 1,200; over 1 million displaced.
